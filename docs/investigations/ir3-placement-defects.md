# IR-3 Generation Defect Analysis

> **Date:** 2026-04-09
> **Scope:** Root-cause analysis of DRC violations caused by IR-3
>   physical placement data generated by the `sonde-hw-design` skill.
> **Source:** KiCad 8.0.9 DRC on carrier board PCB generated from
>   `hw/carrier-board/ir/` by `sonde-kicad`.

---

## Summary

The `sonde-hw-design` skill's Pass 3 generated an IR-3 with connector
positions and zone anchors that produce courtyard overlaps and pad
shorts when realized as actual KiCad footprints. The root causes are
gaps in the skill's placement guidance, not in the IR-3 schema itself.

**7 courtyard overlaps, 3 pad shorts** traced to 6 skill defects.

---

## Defect 1: Courtyard treated as centered bounding box

**Symptom:** Connector courtyards overlap with adjacent components.

**Root cause:** The skill specifies `courtyard_mm: {width, height}`
as a simple width×height pair, and the LLM treats this as a centered
bounding box (±width/2, ±height/2 from the placement position). But
KiCad footprints have **asymmetric** courtyards where the origin is
at pad 1, not at the courtyard center.

**Example:** JST PH 2-pin (`J3`) has courtyard from `(-2.45, -1.85)`
to `(4.45, 6.75)` relative to its origin — the housing extends 6.75mm
in +Y but only 1.85mm in -Y. When the LLM assumes symmetric ±4.30mm,
it underestimates the reach in one direction by 2.45mm.

**Fix needed in skill:** IR-1e should report courtyard as
`courtyard_origin_offset` (min_x, min_y, max_x, max_y) relative to
the footprint origin, not just width×height. Pass 3 must use these
asymmetric bounds when checking clearances.

---

## Defect 2: No rotation-aware clearance checking

**Symptom:** Connectors rotated 90°/270° have courtyard extending in
the wrong axis, causing overlap.

**Root cause:** The skill's Pass 3 instructions say to "perform the
definitive feasibility check using IR-1e courtyard dimensions" but
give no guidance on how rotation transforms the courtyard bounding
box. A 7.80×6.56mm courtyard becomes 6.56×7.80mm when rotated 90°,
and the asymmetric offset rotates with it.

**Fix needed in skill:** Pass 3 instructions must explicitly state
that courtyard clearance checks MUST apply the connector's rotation
transform to the courtyard bounds before checking for overlap with
adjacent components or board edges.

---

## Defect 3: Pin header position ignores pin span

**Symptom:** J6/J7 pin headers extend off the board edge.

**Root cause:** IR-3 specifies J6 at `y_mm: 8.11` described as
"bottom of header (pins 1-7 going upward)." The LLM computed 8.11
as `(35 - 18.80) / 2 ≈ 8.1`, trying to center the header. But with
pin 1 at the origin and 7 pins at 2.54mm pitch, pin 7 is at y=15.24
from the origin. With 180° rotation (pin 1 at bottom), pin 7 is at
`y = 8.11 - 15.24 = -7.13` — **off the board**.

The correct position would be `y_mm ≈ 17.5` so pin 7 lands at
`17.5 - 15.24 = 2.26` (on board, near the top).

**Fix needed in skill:** Pass 3 must require that pin header
positions account for the full pin span in the placement direction.
The instruction should state: "For vertical headers, the position
is the pin-1 anchor; verify that `position.y + pin_span` (or
`position.y - pin_span` for rotated headers) remains within the
board boundary."

---

## Defect 4: Zone anchors inside connector courtyards

**Symptom:** Zone-placed passives (FB1, C1, Q1, R1, R4) overlap
with connectors.

**Root cause:** IR-3 zone anchors are placed without checking
against connector courtyard bounds. For example:

- Zone "power-input" anchor at `(3.0, 10.0)` — inside J3's
  courtyard when J3 is rotated 90° (extends to x=6.75).
- Zone "power-gating" anchor at `(5.0, 14.0)` — inside J6's
  courtyard (extends from x=2.0 to x=5.55).
- Zone "i2c-interface" anchor at `(3.0, 20.0)` — inside J6's
  courtyard (extends up to y≈26mm with 180° rotation).

The LLM placed zone anchors near the associated connectors for
"short trace routing" but didn't verify clearance against the
actual rotated connector courtyards.

**Fix needed in skill:** Pass 3 instructions must require zone
anchor validation: "Before finalizing a zone anchor, verify that
the zone's extent rectangle (anchor + extent_mm) does NOT overlap
with any connector's rotated courtyard bounding box. If overlap
exists, shift the zone anchor until cleared."

---

## Defect 5: No minimum connector-to-connector clearance rule

**Symptom:** J2 (right edge) courtyard overlaps J7 (right pin
header).

**Root cause:** J2 at x=25.0 with courtyard extending ~9.70mm
into the board (to x≈15.3). J7 at x=21.25 with courtyard to
x≈23.0. These overlap because there's no rule requiring clearance
between connectors on the same side of the board.

**Fix needed in skill:** Pass 3 needs an explicit constraint:
"Edge connectors MUST have at least 2mm clearance between their
rotated courtyard bounds and any other component's courtyard.
Validate all connector-to-connector pairs."

---

## Defect 6: Feasibility check uses area ratio only

**Symptom:** IR-3 feasibility check passes (32% utilization) but
the board has 7 courtyard overlaps.

**Root cause:** The skill's feasibility check is:
> "total courtyard area (IR-1e) vs. board area. Warning at >70%
>  utilization; blocking failure at >90%."

This is an **area-based** check — it only asks "do all courtyards
fit on the board?" It does NOT check whether components can be
**arranged** without overlapping. A board with 32% area utilization
can still have overlaps if components are clustered in one region.

**Fix needed in skill:** The feasibility check must add a
**pairwise overlap check**: "After placing all connectors and zone
components, verify that no two component courtyard bounding boxes
overlap. The area ratio check is necessary but not sufficient —
a packing feasibility check is also required."

---

## Recommended Skill Changes

### In IR-1e specification (§ IR-1e)

Add required field:
```yaml
courtyard_bounds:
  min_x: -2.45  # relative to footprint origin
  min_y: -1.85
  max_x: 4.45
  max_y: 6.75
```

This replaces or supplements `courtyard_mm: {width, height}` with
actual asymmetric bounds from the KiCad footprint file.

### In Pass 3 instructions (§ Pass 3)

Add after "Perform the definitive feasibility check":

> **Rotation-aware courtyard checking:** When placing connectors at
> board edges with rotation, transform the courtyard bounds by the
> rotation angle before checking clearances. A courtyard of
> `(-2.45, -1.85, 4.45, 6.75)` rotated 90° becomes
> `(-6.75, -2.45, 1.85, 4.45)` in board coordinates.
>
> **Pin span validation:** For multi-pin connectors (headers, SIPs),
> verify that the full pin span (pin count × pitch) fits within the
> board boundary after rotation. Position + pin span must not exceed
> board dimensions in any direction.
>
> **Pairwise overlap check:** After placing all connectors and
> assigning zone anchors, verify that no two component courtyard
> bounding boxes overlap. Check every connector-connector pair and
> every zone-connector pair. If any overlap exists, adjust positions
> before proceeding.
>
> **Zone anchor clearance:** Zone anchor positions must clear all
> connector courtyards by at least 1mm. The zone extent rectangle
> (anchor + extent_mm) must not intersect any connector's rotated
> courtyard.
